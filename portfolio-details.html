  • Contact: Email for demo / details

Overview

This ongoing PhD research explores convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to assist early detection of Foot-and-Mouth Disease (FMD). The goal is to reduce diagnosis time and support veterinary decision-making by providing probabilistic assessments from clinical imagery under local field conditions.

Objectives

  • Build a robust CNN pipeline tailored to FMD lesion imagery.
  • Evaluate model performance across diverse lighting/devices (field variability).
  • Provide explainable outputs (e.g., Grad-CAM) to aid clinician trust.

Approach

  • Data curation, anonymization, and augmentation (rotation, CLAHE, random crop).
  • Transfer learning baseline (ResNet/EfficientNet), hyperparameter tuning, k-fold validation.
  • Model explainability and error analysis to iteratively reduce false negatives.

Current Status

Model prototyping and dataset preparation are in progress. Next milestones include comparative benchmarking, deployment experiments on low-power devices, and drafting manuscripts for publication.
